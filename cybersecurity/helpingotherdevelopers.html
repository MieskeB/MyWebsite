Helping Other Developers

This chapter is about ways I tried to help other developers increasing the security of their code

CORS

A classmate had a problem with sending requests from his Angular frontend to his Spring Boot API. Since I have a lot of experience in this combination of frontend and backend, he asked me what was wrong. After having a look to the application code, I found out that his CORS configuration was absent. Having worked with Spring Boot, I had a solution ready in one of my older repositories and sent it to him, which solved the problem.

A few weeks later, another classmate asked me a similar question. This time he had a valid CORS configuration. After a lot of debugging and sending the request, I found out that the API didn't accept the extra header that was added to the request. Then the solution was easy: adding exposedHeader to the CORS configuration file.

Setting up Docker Swarm

A classmate told me that he had problems setting up Kubernetes. So I told him that I don't have experience with Kubernetes, but I did have an easy solution: use Docker Swarm. I did warn him that Docker Swarm stated that you should not use it in production mode. But due to lack of time, he decided he wanted to install it anyway.

I helped him with the installation of Docker Swarm. I also helped him with explaining how docker-compose worked and sent him my docker-compose file. He modified my docker-compose file and uploaded it to his own server. From this point everything was working properly.

Reflection

I love to share my own knowledge. Everything I know, I want to help people with. But when I can't solve a problem, I sometimes tend to say that I just don't know it. Afterwards I help them Google some, but I tend to give up sometimes on it. This is something that needs working on in upcoming years.
